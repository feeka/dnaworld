Domain specific language for Bioinformatics? MDSE meets Bioinformatics.

Can we generalize the tasks in bioinfomatics and model them? Majority of tasks in bioinformatics have a very repetitive pattern, so examples are string manipulations(frequent words, reverse complement, median strings, skew problem and e.g.), sequencing using de Bruijn graphs, dynamic programming for alignment and clustering(algorithms like hierarchical clustering). Well, now what we must do for proceeding with this idea is to define our model(I want to note that model is not going to be perfect).

Model

  • Model is formal representation of entities and relationships in real world with certain correspondence for certain purpose.

  • In other words we describe model such that it has:
    • certain abstraction/reduction - removing details that do not serve purpose;
    • projection - statements on model hold for entities from our field(make sense);
    • pragmatics- cann replace its original for specific question.


Bioinformaticians probably have a certain sense of where I am going with this. We are left with one more important question: is this everything that this post was about? Well, no. Interesting part is we want to model the model. In other words we are trying to generalize tasks that are oftenly used by bioinformaticians in order to develop some kind of Domain Specific Language - DSL. Maybe, majority of readers heard about SQL - structured query language. It is a DSL, domain-specific language used in programming and designed for managing data held in a relational database management system (RDBMS).

The correct notion for "modeling the model" is called meta-model. And to me the idea of meta-modeling is very fascinating. Let us give terminology for meta-model.

Meta-model

  • Meta-model precise definition of parts and rules needed to create valid models.

    • How is meta-modeling done? I am going to bring one association of what meta-models are. Take any language in the world: it has syntax and semantics.
